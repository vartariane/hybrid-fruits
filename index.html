Pluot, plumcot, aprium, apriplum, plumcot, plumpicot, pluclot...

They've all got plum and apricot in their lineage, to varying degrees

Many fruits within the Prunus genus—such as plums, apricots, cherries, nectarines, peaches, and almonds—have the same number of chromosomes and share a lot of the same genetic material, so they're pretty easy to cross-pollinate.
