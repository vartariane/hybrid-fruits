A pluot a day makes half the year fly away

The point of fruit is taste. How do pluots measure?

Luckily, Dave Wilson Nursery—which has exclusive rights to sell Pluots®—conducted rigorous, qualitative, and entirely subjective taste tests over 22 years, from 1993-2014. The judges were retail nursery owners and employees, media personnel, radio personalities, garden writers, and members of California Rare Fruit Growers.

Of all fruits tasted, pluots (Flavor King, Dapple Dandy, Flavor Grenade) have been most appealing of all time. 63% of pluots had overall scores in the top 1/3 of all overall scores.

Commercial fruit breeding places huge emphasis on fruit attractiveness. Dapple Dandy, Flavor Supreme, Flavor Grenade, Flavor Queen, Splash, Emerald Drop would have all been in bottom half of attractiveness (rate under 3.6 of 1-5 scale). Dave Wilson Nursery hasn't published attractiveness scores, sadly, but I would have loved to see them.

Attractiveness, ripeness, texture, acid, sugar, and flavor all contribute to top 100 varieties of tested fruits compared to bottom 100, firmness is only parameter that doesn't seem to matter. Tasters tend to give high scores to those with complex character, not just sweet or tart, crunchy or soft. "To have broad appeal, varieties need to excite the palate *and* have pleasant mouth feel"
